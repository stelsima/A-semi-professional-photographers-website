Welcome to my website.

I am an amateur photographer (in the literal sense of the term), and beyond my early solitary exploration of photography, I was fortunate enough to study the medium more systematically at the Photography Department of the Public Vocational Training Institute of Chania. Subsequently, I became certified through national exams by EOPPEP (the National Organization for the Certification of Qualifications and Vocational Guidance).

I also hold a degree from the Statistics Department of the University of Piraeus and make a living working in the broader maritime sector, employed by a public organization.

I can’t recall when I first fell in love with photography, as the photographic process has now become an almost involuntary biological need for me. On this site, as an antidote to the relentless passage of time, I attempt to share images from some of my photographic series that have preoccupied me, or more simply, I attempt to share with you my own little stories. After all, as the African-American poet Maya Angelou said, “There is no greater agony than bearing an untold story inside you.”

I hope that in turn, you find these stories interesting and that they manage to convey to you some of their emotional weight. I will gladly welcome your constructive comments as part of my journey in exploring the cultural asset known as the photographic medium, always believing that “cultural goods are the only goods that, when shared, enrich us.”

The technical analysis, design, and implementation of this website were done by my son, Stelios Simantirakis, a senior student of computer engineering.
